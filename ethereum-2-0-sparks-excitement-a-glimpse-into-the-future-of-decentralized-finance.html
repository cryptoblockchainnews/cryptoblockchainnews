With the introduction of a new Proof-of-Stake (PoS) consensus mechanism, Ethereum 2.0 resolves scalability issues by allowing network participants to stake their holdings and contribute to the validation process. Consequently, transaction speeds will increase, and the network will be capable of processing a tremendous number of transactions simultaneously. By actively staking Ethereum, participants become validators and are rewarded with additional tokens for their contributions. This economic incentive encourages more significant network participation while diminishing the environmental impact typically associated with Proof-of-Work (PoW) consensus mechanisms. Enthusiasm for Ethereum grows as decentralized finance (DeFi) platforms continue to gain popularity. The innovative DeFi ecosystem allows users to lend, borrow, trade, and earn interest through blockchain-powered applications.
