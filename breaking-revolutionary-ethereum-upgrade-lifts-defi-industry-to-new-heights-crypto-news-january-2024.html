Scalability: The Key to Mass Adoption Scalability has been a persistent challenge for Ethereum, causing network congestion, slower transactions, and higher fees. However, Ethereum 2.0 introduces a novel solution called "sharding." By implementing sharding, the Ethereum network can process a significantly higher number of transactions simultaneously, minimizing congestion and providing a seamless user experience. This enhancement opens doors for broader adoption of DeFi applications among the masses, fostering growth and innovation in the crypto industry. Transition to Proof-of-Stake (PoS) Another noteworthy aspect of Ethereum 2.0 is its transition from the energy-intensive Proof-of-Work (PoW) algorithm to the more eco-friendly Proof-of-Stake (PoS) consensus. PoS relies on validators who hold and "stake" their Ethereum as collateral to secure the network and validate transactions. This move not only drastically reduces energy consumption but also promotes a more decentralized system where participants with higher stakes have a greater say in the network's governance.
