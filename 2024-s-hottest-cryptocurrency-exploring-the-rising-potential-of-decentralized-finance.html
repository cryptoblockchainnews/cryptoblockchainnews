Notably, the rise of decentralized exchanges (DEXs) has played a pivotal role in popularizing DeFi. Traditional centralized exchanges often face security vulnerabilities and require users to trust a central authority. However, DEXs eliminate these concerns by utilizing smart contracts, which are self-executing agreements running on the blockchain. This gives users greater control over their funds and eliminates the risk of hacks or manipulation. Moreover, the introduction of yield farming has taken the DeFi industry by storm. Yield farming allows users to generate passive income by lending or staking their cryptocurrencies, typically in the form of governance tokens, on DeFi platforms. This innovative approach has attracted immense attention, propelling DeFi platforms to offer enticing rewards to lure users on board.
